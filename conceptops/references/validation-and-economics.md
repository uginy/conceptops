# Validation, Economics, and Quality

## Research Integrity

- Prefer primary and authoritative sources.
- Browse for claims likely to change.
- Record retrieval dates for research artifacts.
- Cite important current claims near the claim.
- Distinguish facts, estimates, hypotheses, assumptions, and opinions.
- Do not invent precision, traction, customers, quotes, partnerships, credentials, or test results.
- Use formulas or code for arithmetic; use models to choose and explain assumptions.

## Decision Gate

Score only dimensions relevant to the task, using 1 to 5 as a decision aid rather than measured truth:

- problem strength;
- evidence quality;
- differentiation;
- reachable distribution;
- economic potential;
- execution feasibility.

Return:

- `Go`: evidence supports a narrow next step;
- `Conditional`: a decisive assumption needs a cheap experiment;
- `Freeze`: evidence does not justify further investment.

For `Conditional`, define the experiment, pass/fail thresholds, cost, time, and next decision. For `Freeze`, state what evidence could reopen the idea.

## Cheapest Decisive Experiment

Prefer the smallest experiment that can invalidate the riskiest assumption. Define:

- hypothesis;
- target participant, system, or dataset;
- procedure;
- observable signal;
- pass threshold;
- fail or kill threshold;
- expected cost and duration;
- decision after each outcome.

Avoid vanity metrics and experiments that cannot change the decision.

## Unit Economics

Adapt the model to the business type. Do not force subscription metrics onto transaction, marketplace, licensing, or service models.

Use transparent inputs and formulas:

- revenue = paying units * average revenue per unit;
- contribution per unit = revenue per unit - variable cost per unit;
- contribution margin = contribution per unit / revenue per unit;
- break-even units = fixed monthly costs / contribution per unit;
- payback periods = acquisition cost / contribution per customer per period;
- simple ROI = (benefit - investment) / investment.

Use conservative, base, and upside scenarios. Show units, source or rationale, confidence, and sensitivity to the two most decisive assumptions. Never invent precise acquisition cost, conversion, churn, market share, or growth inputs.
